The Shotgun

Spring ‘24

Of all the things you can believe in, your own success is the most incredible one, given you were expecting failure. With most odds stacked against you, remembering some are actually for you proves to be difficult. After all, when facing 300.000, what difference does it make if there are 3 or 300 of you. So the relief I felt having successfully passed all of the exams during my first semester is left up to interpretation.

My first proper break allowed me to finally go home and spend time with my family and friends after a long while. For most of them it was the same old same old. Some had new jobs, friends and even significant others. Exciting stuff, learning how life's been treating the same people I’ve grown up with, given nothing’s happened to me anyway.

I’ve known Franceska, and most of my closest friends, for as long as I’ve known myself. Not knowing what she had planned, I was expecting nigh anything, but was nonetheless surprised to see boyfriend alongside her. She’d planned to introduce us for a while now, so I eagerly approached them, making the acquaintance.

My gaze shifted to the girl standing next to both of them: one of her peers I assumed. ”Leah, I think we’ve been introduced before,” she said. Greeting her, I recalled us being on the line alongside Franceska and a few other of her contemporaries during a call about 2 months ago. I never expected to meet any of them, but that was a pleasant surprise. They invited me to their go-to hangout spot, where we spent the next few hours. We chatted a bit and I got to learn more about them. Ceska and her beau huddled up on the couch, so Leah and I mostly played together.

Time flew by and we were already bidding one another farewell, but just before that Leah told me she’s been getting into a game that I myself have spent a few hours too many playing, and so we agreed upon reaching out and playing together one day, and so we did. An occurrence turned routine, we’d spend more and more time together.

As we were on call as usual, she suddenly got anxious. She told me she randomly received a text message from her ex, and so the call came to an abrupt end. I was surprised she’d be so candid about it, never assuming she'd deem us close enough. Unsure of what to do, I called it a day only to wake up to a drunk text where she told me how she felt: how the whole situation hurt her, how she was emotionally destabilized because of it, how she felt ashamed in front of me. I think that was the only time I’ve seen her make typos in her texts. This sudden surge of trust and vulnerability touched me, and I tried to support her as best I could.

She’d tell me of her life, I’d tell her a bit of mine. Suddenly the sandcastle’s walls were washed away by the tide, and we just knew each other, a recent acquaintance turned old friend overnight. I integrated her into my life, introducing her to another friend I often played with. We’d meet regularly, often entering Sandman’s embrace with the first morning rays, and life was beautiful.

That friend had a girlfriend, so when we spoke of our pastime our friends would joke that he’s found himself a mistress. I objected, feeling guilty that her name was being dragged, until I stopped to think about why a harmless joke wouldn’t sit right with me. Was I falling for her?

A short break meant coming home for a short while. That meant meeting with Leah. I was riddled with my thoughts, unsure of how I felt: this was the first time since my messy breakup almost 20 months back that I’ve thought I felt this way. Was this the truth or mere delusion? I had one too many questions and one too few answers, so I became conscious of all my actions. This would be the first time we met tête-à-tête, and I feared coming on too strong, so I asked if anyone else would be coming along. When she said she’d be the only one I felt relief, as I really wanted to spend the time together. Alas I was compelled to ask once more for the sake of my peace of mind when we did eventually meet. The answer had not changed.

I suggested we took a walk, and it really was a walk to remember. Never expected it to come to this, to find her mere presence a gift of its own. I have no recollection whatsoever of what transpired underway, what dialogue accompanied us on our trek. I only remember having a blast talking to her and then finally arriving at our destination and playing pool. I believe I lost every single game, save for maybe one, but the cue’s trajectory was the last thing on my mind back then. I vividly remember a peculiar occurrence: some guys were playing on the neighboring table, and whenever she’d shoot from that side of the table I’d stand behind her so as not to let them see her bending. I was unsure if I was overdoing it and imagining things, but as they left I caught their gaze on me and one of them nodded. I nodded back, dumbfounded by why he’d do that: it felt like that scene from Fantastic Mr. Fox, where he and a wolf both raised their fists. He may have been an acquaintance from 9 months ago whom I vaguely remembered. Or maybe he noticed what I was doing and decided to let me know he respected it. Whatever it was, that man left never to appear in my life again, but the memory of our brief interaction will live on for as long as the memory of Leah does.

After a good game of pool, what could have possibly been better than a lunch at a Georgian place? You can never go wrong with khinkali, and so true romance was born. After that we only met once more before I went back for uni, and that time she invited me out with Franceska and Mike, just like the first time. I remember how we joked after the latter two turned down the proposal to go to the Georgian place again, and so we settled on sushi. Delightfully devilish, Leah, you’d given me a “Love Is” gum wrap under the pretense that you already had the same one. Don’t think that went unnoticed, I just thought further flirting would make it awkward. How nice it was. Afterwards, some of their friends joined and we went to the café where they’d usually spend all their time. I got to witness their routine, but that really was of second importance at the moment. The pleasantries ended when Ceska and Mike left and we followed suit shortly afterwards, but what would a mention-worthy date be if not for a major development? As we “ciao”-ed and went our separate ways, she called my name.
“We won’t be seeing each other for a while after this since you’re leaving, right?”
“That’s right, my flight is in 2 days,” I responded only to see her coming my way. Suddenly, we hugged goodbye, since saying it wouldn’t be enough. A warm smile and a bubble gum wrap’s what I was left with.

Summer ‘24

“Wow, it’s 2 AM already… I really should be hanging up now…”
“But do you really have to?”
That single question was the reason we’d spend the next few days falling asleep on call. The minute I was back from uni was the minute I’d ring, and the UI had no “end call” button. We’d spend days on end just talking, playing games or sitting in comfortable silence, contemplating each other’s presence. So this is what being content was, how could anyone ever ask for more? This girl I met, this little side product of chance… Now, one of the most tender things in my life. Gather my fondest, most cherished memories in a flask, and you will surely see me waking up that day, seeing her profile picture with a call runtime of 9:41:37. She was there in the morning, she did not hang up overnight.

Falling asleep meant waking up “next to her”, so there was something to look forward to. I would open my eyes and just stare at her profile picture. I would gently whisper her name to check if she was still asleep. At some point, I’d hear a sleepy “hmm?”, and we would talk for a bit before I had to get going. I remember how one time she started a video call while still in her nightgown. I was so embarrassed, I did not know if I was allowed to see her like this or if she was just too sleepy to comprehend what was going on.

She spent so much time with me, I wonder if she had time for her friends? I never cared to check if I had time for my friends, but none reached out, so none must have been discontent. Living in an empty apartment for almost two years including college does take its toll on one’s psyche, and finally having something… someone to come back to was refreshing, rejuvenating, invigorating. I had found the person to accompany me on this lonesome road, someone who fit like they were kin.

“So, what are your favorite flowers?” I asked, and in 2 minutes an order for “Lego Tulips” was sent out. Weeks later, the same was done to a “Lego Adidas Superstar” box. Apparently, I was now trustworthy enough to be shown some notes in the childhood diary.

“Dear Diary, we are moving soon, so I have compiled a list of things I want.”

“Item #1: Furniture,” I burst out laughing, asking if she’s been sleeping on cold hardwood flooring this entire time. Amusing to see a young teen think of furniture as the thing they want.

“Item #2: Stabilo Highlighters. Item #3: Adidas Superstar”

I asked her what they looked like since I was never one to be knowledgeable in brands. She then told me she never got to buy them, and we joked about how missing out on her favorite shoes must have been a hard experience. The rest is history.

I still remember the time I woke up to a cranefly in the kitchen. Totally harmless, possibly cute to some, nothing but terrifying to me. Look at its sleek, slim build; the thin, long legs. It could have modeled, had it not been an active hazard of eggs being laid in my ear at night. No matter if it was total nonsense, fear was in the driver’s seat with objectivity taking a much needed beauty sleep right beside it. I remember being on call with her, freaking out about the insect, wanting to kill it but then settling for a more humane approach. One does not simply capture a cranefly on the ceiling, elevation needed be gained. Good thing countertops were a thing, else the bug would not have been evacuated. Bad thing I am so clumsy, else the bug would have all 6 legs intact. Turns out forcing it under a glass on a piece of paper is easier said than done, but at the very least his life had been spared. Funny to think how her keeping company turned this trivial chore into something to write about a year and a half later.

“Do you wanna watch a movie sometime?”

You bet I do, the hell I wouldn’t!
“Sure thing, what do you have in mind?”

“You remember how we discussed Lynch’s first movie, “Erase Head”, right?”

I will never forget the satisfaction of her seeing the name for herself and admitting I’m right in that it was in fact called “Eraserhead”. We didn’t really enjoy the movie, but the experience made it all worthwhile. Things couldn’t have been going better, admittance of feeling imminent. I was only worried I would do something wrong, so I had better decide on everything before it was too late. Only it wouldn’t be

“Anyway, I’ll be on my way. I’m already late.”

“I’ll be hearing from you, bye!”

“Love you, bye!” The sound of the call being ended denied me my right to ask her what was just said. Grinning like an idiot, I lingered in the call for some moments, savoring the echo of her words in the confines of my headphones. Was that a slip, or did she mean that casually? Maybe she’s British, maybe that’s how she talks to everyone. And maybe she did love me. No matter the reason, hearing that was enough to light up my chest. I hoped she’d call soon, I missed her already.

“Sorry, I gotta go. I agreed on watching a movie with someone”, the first reality check so far. I am adamant that the misunderstanding lies on my part, because there is no way there is someone else like me in her life, right? She has no free time to manage another person, or so I supposed. But you could suppose anything, suppose the whole building fell down.

The exact conversation evaded being etched in my memory, alas the gut wrenching shock persists. How could I've been so blind and full of myself?

I messed up. I was planning out my vacation weeks without actually being hired. What a fool, and a confident one at that. Of course, I didn’t start a fight over that, considering the only fault here was my own. She never actually said or promised anything, I could have been misinterpreting everything up to this point. So why was my stomach churning?

“What do you think of “Trauma-dumping”? Well, remember that guy…”
Divine intervention. The boy was nothing but a sham, she got bored of him in a matter of days. A wave of relief washed over me like a cold shower after spending the day under the blazing sun. The embers of unease were extinguished, preventing a wildfire.

The experience made me more self aware, however my observations did not change: I was still convinced there was something beautiful blooming between us, there was no other explanation. I never spent a moment to recollect my thoughts and consider the fact that the jackass should never have appeared in the first place.

Why was he the jackass? He has less of a clue about my existence than I do about his, and even so, why would I be a stopping factor in his aim for Leah? Shouldn’t I be mad at her? Wasn’t it her who’s been leading me for the entire time? Oh no, what am I saying? Not Leah, there's no reason to put any blame on her. Better to swipe this under the rug.

“I’m dating Baker,” The rug might hide dust, but a sandstorm it cannot handle. Not a month had passed since the boring guy’s departure, when suddenly she started telling me of some mutual friend of hers. How they would meet once in a while, how he taught her tennis, how I have been ignoring this only to paint a picture of myself that bore no fruit.

Good for you: keep your unworthy mouth shut, so she sees how supportive you are. Don’t speak up for herself, lest she thinks you’re an ass. Eat it up and don’t think about spitting: who do you think the janitor is? Remember everything you’ve learned since the breakup, the years of change? They never actually happened, you’re just as much a slave for feelings as you ever were.

Weeks went by, listening and being happy for her. I didn’t have to do anything, I explicitly chose to do so in hopes that I would receive the same output from her. Most peculiar thing: the dynamic between us didn’t seem to shift that much. There are weeks where nothing happens, and there are nights where fates are sealed:

“Imagine, you know someone and you have a thing for them for a while now. You are sure it’s reciprocated, then suddenly they start dating someone-”, I stopped abruptly, praying she wouldn’t put as much thought in this as I did. Why the hell did I just ask her about the literal situation we are in?

“Chuck, at this point I have to ask,” my prayers had not been answered, as I knew exactly what the question was. I had to divert the conversation, I had to do it quickly and be subtle about it. Luckily, she left a pause between her words.

“I was just thinking about that situation we discussed last week, remember? I was wondering how you’d act, what you’d feel.” a shot in the dark can still hit the bullseye. We dropped the topic and the discussion went on, but I experienced shellshock. I spent the next few days turning this conversation over in my head to the point where it became perfectly round. Why would she be asking me anything, why would she care for that? And she knew the answer, the question was just a formality: what would she do with it? Yes, Leah, I am into you, and yes, I am dissatisfied with the existence of Baker, now what? The answer came suddenly.

“I broke up with him,” the second time this summer my soul experienced the soothing effects of hope based balm. Reasons be whatever they are, I have met the optimal outcome. Just days ago my empty stare drilled a hole in the tulips and the shoes I bought, thinking how stupid it was to rush and buy them months before her birthday. Now, the pride in the choice of presents has been reinstated and life was beautiful again. But if I learned anything, it’s that time was of the essence.

It was a regular night, but I have been preparing for it. I drew out an entire script, how I would start the discussion, wrote down questions and topics I would like to go over. Showtime:

“You know, one of the things I like about that story is how everything in it points to him being the rat, but it is never explicitly confirmed. He denies it until the bitter end, and there is no letter or recording that would prove it. There is still this small understatement that just doesn’t sit right with the overall narrative. But I want to avoid such understatements, so I have to go back to a topic we left unexplored. Remember that question you wanted to ask me? I’ve been meaning to ask you that very same question myself”

“I understand. I think this was to be expected. I cannot answer it audibly, so I’ll write it down,” she said, demonstrating her screen to me. Letter after letter, the answer was drawn out but given:

“The thing is that the answer to this question that you have just asked has been positive for a while now.” my heart would fracture my rib if not calmed soon. I am understanding this correctly, right? “Has been” means it still is, not that it stopped being that, right? I forgot all the English I knew. She confirmed it.

I felt the light shining from above. A glorious symphony of triumph was being played, and it was all for me. I am the winner and there is nothing that could derail me. We talked and flirted for so long, I could not fall asleep out of sheer joy. I couldn’t believe this was happening, but I prayed this was real. Happiness occurs regularly, be it small things or big. But it rarely feels like a golden stream filling your chest and whispering: your life is fulfilling and you are the champion. That girl was mine and I were hers, it was just a matter of time before we cemented it.

That time would never come, washed away by the waves of distant behavior and the tides of doubt. Not a day later, she would send me a handwritten letter, saying that I ruined everything because it will never be the same. What shocked me most, however, was the statement that I held her vulnerable sides against her. When discussing the future, I wanted to go over everything that could be relevant for us, which included the sensitive topics of her past. Should I have not mentioned them, or was I too crude when doing so? I never meant to sound judgemental, I just wanted to be clear on the way I felt… but I knew how it would sound, so why did I go through with it? I am such an asshole, and feeling sorry isn’t even the least I could do. I tried talking about it and explaining what I meant, but it didn’t seem to affect much, especially since she just said that sending me this letter was a mistake. Was it really a rash decision or was she just trying to avoid discussing this further? Unease now stopped creeping up and entered a full sprint, covering me whole. The beginning of the fall, ever so elegant and tragic.

One thing that kept me sane was the recollection of our confessions:

“Remember that time you accidentally said “Love you” when leaving the call?”

“Haha, yes. I covered my face and nearly died from embarrassment.”

“And about that time we met in spring, I tried acting like I insisted someone tag along. I was too embarrassed to actually ask you out on a one-on-one”

“I remember being disappointed, thinking you didn't want to spend time alone with me. It was around this time that I developed feelings for you”

“Oh well, Mrs. Leah- I mean Ms. Leah,”

“Quite ahead of yourself, aren't you?”

But the difficulty of keeping up the act rose proportionally to her brevity and hostility. I felt her grow distant, disinterested in me. Telling each other about our days was routine by now, so her being ticked off by it came as a surprise, to say the least.

“I just fail to understand you. Why are you telling me this? Nothing awful's happened, there are things much worse than that.”

“What is there to understand? Haven't we been doing this the entire Summer?”

So what if the practical course isn’t that difficult, am I not supposed to talk about it anymore? I told you my building’s elevator broke down so I had to bring my luggage up two flights of stairs, and all you could muster was, “at least it’s not the 10th floor.”

Brava, my dear, brava!

What caused this sudden shift? For the longest time, our conversations are nothing but pleasantries, then we confess our feelings, causing the dynamic to make a sharp U-turn. Was I being too sensitive due to expecting courtship or was there merit to my feelings? Did we manage to exit the candy-bouquet phase without ever dating? There was no way to know, and so I tried my best to stay on good terms.

Once, on my way home, I saw a truck that said “Baker”.

Fall ‘24

As a kid, sleep was never troublesome. It was lights out whenever my parents told me so, and the only time I could not drift off was on the night before New Year's Eve: that's when we received gifts under the tree, not on Christmas. As time went by, it became wobbly. I remember the first time I stayed up until 4 AM, texting with a friend of mine; the first all-nighter I pulled, a cherished memory of 10 people on a school retreat. And I remember how I couldn't fall asleep the night before her birthday.

“You have to be there, or the celebration is cancelled,” how cute of her to insist on my attendance. Perhaps I was wrong after all. I came home a day before her birthday, the timing couldn’t have been better. Some of us were supposed to meet at Franceska’s and then go to the venue together, since Leah had work that day. I lay in bed, trying to fall asleep, but my mind was racing.

I was recounting all the presents I had packed: it’s been a while since romantic feelings were involved in the gifting process. The aforementioned Lego sets, a Slipknot Tee, some Invisibobbles, some BIC pens and mechanical pencils and of course, the one and only: Milka brownies. At one point she'd list stuff she wanted me to bring. She then told me she was joking, but that's just something you have to say after asking for half a dozen items.

It felt like something was missing: not a main ingredient, but a final touch, something to top it all off. The cherry on top, something so necessary I was surprised I could ever forget it: a letter! The fragile care put into using old media to communicate. And so I took out a blank piece of paper, a pen and so I started writing. Looking back, it may have been cheesy and corny, but I did pour my soul into it. I was overwhelmed with emotion, excited for the future, reminiscing of the summer that’s barely ended. I had some blank space at the end, so I added in a few doodles.

The day had just begun, but I could not contain my excitement. I was at Ceska’s, Mike was there too. We hung out waiting for the others, and then we set off. The charm quickly wore off as we got stuck in traffic, when Leah’s closest friend suggested I go there by foot since we were already close by, because they had to swing by the confectionery and get the cake. I gladly agreed, getting there first would mean some time alone. I remember the warm feeling, thinking that since this was suggested by someone who knew Leah best, this was probably what would make Leah happiest. I rushed to the meeting point and got there first, but I didn’t have to wait long for Leah. I was so happy, seeing her for the first time since Spring. Even though Summer lasted so long and gave us so many memories, I never got the chance to come home during that time.

“This is for you,” she said to my surprise. She went out of her way and got me a gift: it was a comic I suggested we read together. The first page bore an inscription:

“From Leah; To Charles”

She told me she showed it to her favorite coworker today. That had no deeper meaning, that was just something she did, but I believe I found it adorable, thinking this gift must have meant so much to her she decided to show it to someone important. It’s funny how romantic feelings can permutate the context of any action, adding thought and care to something done on a whim. Maybe that is what makes it so beautiful, caring for someone and interpreting everything as their care for you.

We didn’t get much time alone, as some of the other quests came early as well, and it wasn’t long before the cake squad made the delivery. I planned on placing the letter in her handbag so she’d open it later as a surprise, and at some point I asked her if she was going to be holding onto it through the entire evening. I remember her smirking and letting go of it, so I thought she’d figured out what was going on. We ate, chatted, watched her blow the candles: the complete ritual, while I sat right beside her.

“We saw the way he looked at you,” I recalled, “his eyes were filled with love.” It was something we discussed back in the Summer, when she told me something her friends said after the hangout in late Spring.

Suddenly, her phone rang. It was an unknown number, she picked it up and started talking. They seemed to know each other, but she was not happy the person on the line called. She insisted they stopped it and that there was no point, but despite the hostility in her words she kept smiling and laughing. I assumed it was an ex, and that made me uncomfortable: why would she spend her time talking to him on her birthday, surrounded by her friends, sitting next to me? I discussed it with her later, and she agreed it was wrong to keep the guests waiting for such an awkward matter. Apparently it was some weirdo from high school who’d harassed her previously - can’t think of a single reason not to hang up immediately after finding out who it was.

Nonetheless, the evening went on, and I was glad she liked the presents, even if she knew what half of them would be. At one point, while they bantered, I snuck out and settled the bill. I came back to them, and after a while they asked for the check, only to find out it’s been paid. Seeing as it raised too many questions, I came forward and confessed only to see her eyes become saddened.

I recalled how she told me of her first tattoo, thinking she may have felt in a similar way back then. It was a day before our confessions, and she sent me a picture of it: it was a lyric from a song she liked, written on her lateral superior left abdomen. I’m not sure if she called me or the entire dialogue took place via text, but she asked me if I did not like it because I sounded disappointed. I never knew how I felt about tattoos: I never had anything against them, but I was always weirded out by the idea of having one. In turn, this feeling must have been projected on people I consider peers and care deeply about, since at the moment I did not know how or what to feel. But I explained it, and told her it looked wonderful. I was glad the misunderstanding’s been cleared without unnecessary drama, but I remember the concern I felt in that question and how it was similar to the one she was expressing right now.

We had a brief discussion about it, and settled on her treating me at the Georgian restaurant so we’d be even. Her brother would be picking her and some of the quests up, and I helped her carry the presents all the way to the road. I asked her if it was okay for me to stay and wait for them to get into the car. At first, she didn’t object, but then she said it might be weird for her brother to see me. Not questioning her decision, I hugged her one last time, said goodbye to everyone else and moved away about 15 meters. I called a taxi to that spot so I’d be able to keep an eye on them before they got in the car while not drawing any attention to myself. Overall, the entire ordeal had been successful, and I was satisfied.

A couple of days later she called me. A friend was staying over that night, and I left the room to answer the phone. I was so glad she’d called, but the gleefulness was wiped away in seconds by her tone. I was not sure why she was so glum, so she spoke:

“I don’t want to give you any false expectations,” which is great, except I am unsure of what this is supposed to mean. I cannot recall what the exact phrasings were, but it was so similar to a breakup call I was stupefied for a moment. When I asked her what was wrong, or rather what could theoretically be wrong for the expectations she’d given me not to be met, I didn’t get a single coherent answer. At first, my age was the problem: apparently she’d wanted someone older, and me being 2.5 years younger was a real roadblock. Funny she’d figured it out only now, after half a year of being close friends. I argued that since we were both of legal age this gap was too small to matter, especially because it didn’t translate into a mental age gap - I was met with another one of my hamartias: I wasn’t tall, or rather, the same height as her. I’m sorry, dear, I lost the extra centimeters in a game of poker!

After she hung up I was too disoriented to understand what exactly had happened: was I out of the woods since I’d defended against all of her nitpicks, or were those just poor excuses that she tried to use to justify her bailing? I was lost, but it did not matter. I had 2 more days before I had to leave, and tomorrow we’d both be at Mike’s small concert.

I shouldn’t have bothered waiting, since she just acted like we didn’t really know each other. She smiled when she saw me, and that was it: she avoided conversation at all cost. I only asked her if she picked up smoking again, since she told me she’d quit for good. Silly question, the cigarette butt smothered by her shoe was enough of an answer. Ceska and Mike were the only ones to speak to me that night, and she must have noticed I felt weird: she later told me she asked Leah if she knew what’s up. Apparently, Leah told her we “spoke” on the phone, and that must have been why I was down. Partially, yes, but it had more to do with her denying us ever knowing each other, as if I were a mere shadow from her past. Quite a show, this shadow was supposed to be her present and future half a week ago. The only thing she’d told us is that she had some business to take care of before she left. She knew it would be the last time we saw each other before I left, and she would’ve just walked out had I not stopped her, spreading my hands out. She accepted the embrace, bidding me farewell, leaving me none the wiser.

The last time I saw her that evening was when I was walking away from the venue: she’d departed just minutes before me and I saw her with the same girl that was at the concert, but as I waved at them they did not seem to notice. One should never attribute to malice what can be attributed to ignorance, but I felt like they ignored me on purpose.

It was a silly situation, where I had two weeks of lab work in the middle of my break, with the first week only having 2 days busy. So as not to waste any time in the lecture free period, I decided to travel somewhere close to where I studied: coming home for some 4 days was not worth the journey it took. She’d been vanishing in the last few days, slowly but steadily. The little adventure helped me take my mind off of it, but the problem never went away: I figured I’ve waited long enough to rule out the possibility that I was just overreacting, and so I fired away:

“What happened, do you want to stop talking to me?” Maybe there was something better I could have said, but at the moment her behavior left me in a stupor.

“I’ve read your letter and I didn’t like anything in it, so yeah.” The bell rang. The towel had no time to be thrown in, I was already knocked out by the opponent: I died so close to the goal, one would wonder: did I do it with a smile? Was I happy leaving this world, knowing I came so close to greatness? I should have left it at that, but I couldn’t help but ask her more questions, requesting her to respond so then I’d leave her alone. I got no response, and that was it.

When I came home for the rest of the break I did not contact her nor did I hear from her first. I remember Ceska telling me she was trying to set her up with a friend of Mike’s and I was… dumbfounded. I did not confront her as there was nothing she’d done wrong, but I still felt unwell. Why would she do it? And why would she tell me about it? In the end, I never contacted her and so break ended and the new semester began.

I would still often think of her, and so one day my mind wandered to that time she showed me a Rubic’s cube her ex gave her. She joked that her shelves were just galleries of gifts from exes, and that memory made me shudder: was that who I was destined to become? “Yet another”, “one of many”, someone who would sometimes be remembered by sarcastic remarks about my gifts?

Why is it that when I finally stop and try to fall asleep, I just hope for her to text me for no reason at all? Why do I still expect the vibrations on my phone to be her?

I am lost and I feel hollow. I don't feel a continuous emotion, but rather spontaneous outbursts. I am grieving the loss of her. Despite this, I am happy I got to know and love her.

I really hope she finds peace and happiness, that she is truly happy and did not just convince herself she is. Her declining the digital gift that I scheduled for New Year’s day just before I found out was a beautiful stop in front of the sentence that we were. —

Writing was a strange part of Charles’ life. At a young age a teacher complimented his style, and so the idea never left his head. He started out a couple of times, only to drop it after at most finishing a single chapter. Funny enough, he later returned to the abandoned craft just to sext with his then girlfriend. But it must have sparked something in him, since he wrote more and more often after that. His breakup was one of his greatest inspirations for writing: he drafted multiple texts with the idea of expressing and sharing his pain through words, but nothing ever came out of it. He then dropped the idea of turning it into a book, and would episodically write sad little monologues whenever something bad happened. And now, the shock and trauma were strong enough to push him to his limit. As Charles thought of how to end this short story, the best he could come up with was reflecting by breaking the 4th wall.

After typing the last sentence, Charles went to sleep, not knowing what to expect next.
